The Day I Realized We've Been Using the Internet Wrong

Why your best insights from web research vanish—and what to do about it

July 22, 2025 • 5 min read

I was preparing our quarterly competitive analysis. Twenty-three tabs open across competitor sites. Notion, Airtable, Linear, Monday—I was deep in their pricing pages, feature lists, customer testimonials.

I found it. The perfect pricing model on a competitor's site. Three tiers, usage-based, with a genius approach to enterprise pricing. I highlighted key sections. Made mental notes. Took screenshots.

Three weeks later, board meeting prep. "Which competitor had that innovative pricing model you mentioned?"

I spent 47 minutes searching. Browser history showed 312 sites visited that day. The screenshots? Unlabeled in a folder with 200+ others. My insights about why their model was genius? Gone.

Why Current Methods Fail

Bookmarks: "Competitor Research" folder with 847 links. No context about why you saved them.
Screenshots: Desktop folder with 200+ images named "Screenshot 2024-11-15 at 2.34.17 PM"
Note-taking apps: Copy-pasting loses the visual context, the exact location, the surrounding information
Spreadsheets: Great for data, useless for visual insights, UX patterns, or design decisions

The problem isn't organization. The problem is that your insights are separated from their source.
