The Pattern That Traps Us All

Most browsing isn't discovery; it's revisiting‚Äîcoming back to pages you've already seen to keep working. Browser history, tabs, and bookmarks are fine for where you were, but not for what you learned, so you rebuild context again and again. People even keep huge tab piles as "reminders," which taxes attention and makes closing anything feel risky.

What Research Shows

  • Revisits are a large share of browsing; history/bookmarks don't match how people return
  • Tabs are used as reminders; overload adds cognitive cost

Sources: Tauscher & Greenberg 1997; Adar, Teevan, Dumais 2009; CHI'21 CMU
